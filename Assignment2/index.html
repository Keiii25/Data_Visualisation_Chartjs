Climate Change
How is it affecting the globe?

Visualising a warmer world

Temperature changes has been increasing gradually across the globe especially in least developing countries. 2020 was the second warmest year on record since 1850, followed 2016. The global mean temperature in 2020 is estimated to have been 1.27 °C (2.29 °F) above the average temperature of the late 19th century, from 1850-1900, a period often used as a pre-industrial baseline for global temperature targets.

Global Temperature Anomaly of Land and Ocean

The earth has become warmer compared to the 1951 to 1980 average. Warmer temperatures over time are changing weather patterns and disrupting the usual balance of nature. This poses many risks to human beings and all other forms of life on Earth.

The impacts of Climate Change on Mortality

Climate change is responsible for the rise of natural disasters such as flood, wildfires, droughts, ocean acidification and etc.. All these events has disrupted the lives of people which in turns causes deaths. There is a positive correlation between the rise of temperature and number of deaths per 1000 in the world. This relationship is especially evident in Europe.

Greenhouse Gas Composition

Greenhouse gas is the main issue that is causing global warming as these gases get traps within the atmosphere and prevents the heat from escaping the atmosphere. Carbon dioxide is accountable for 79% of the greenhouse gases.

Carbon Dioxide Emission

As the top producers of fossils fuels in the world, Australia, United States and Russia are among the highest contributors to carbon dioxide emissions. These countries depends heavily on fossils fuel for source of electricity and exportation.
About 60% of Australia’s electricity over the past year came from burning coal thus contributing to high emissionn of carbon dioxide. Developing countries contributes to more than half of the carbon dioxide emission in the world. (Morton, 2021)
